About

I am currently a Consultant (Grade I) at the National Centre for Good Governance (NCGG), Ministry of Personnel, Public Grievances and Pensions, Government of India, based in New Delhi. I work on evaluation and strengthening of public service delivery systems, including Right to Service (RTS) Acts and digital governance portals across Indian states.

I completed my Ph.D. in Economics at the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Indore, Department of Humanities and Social Sciences, under the supervision of Dr. Kalandi Charan Pradhan. My doctoral research focuses on internal migration, urban exposure, and later-life health outcomes among older adults in India, using data from large-scale surveys such as LASI and other national datasets.

Research

Later-life migration & successful ageing

How migration histories and urban exposure shape physical health, functional limitations, depression, and subjective wellbeing among older adults in India.

Urban exposure & obesity

Investigating how timing, duration, and intensity of urban residence influence obesity and cardiometabolic risks among migrants in mid and later life.

Mental health & disability

Examining how functional limitations, social exclusion, and access to care interact to produce inequalities in late-life depression.

Governance & service delivery

Evaluating RTS Acts, digital portals, and grievance redress mechanisms using administrative data and mixed-methods designs.

Conceptually, my work is informed by life-course perspectives, cumulative (dis)advantage, and the capability approach, linking early- and mid-life opportunities to health and wellbeing in older age.

Policy & Consultancy Work

At NCGG, I contribute to evaluation studies and policy support for Union and State governments. Recent work includes:

  • Supporting evaluations of national skill development and public-service schemes.
