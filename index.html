About Me

I am an economist and population researcher working at the intersection of internal migration, ageing, and health inequalities in India. I combine large-scale ageing surveys such as LASI and NFHS with life-course and cumulative (dis)advantage perspectives to understand how migration trajectories, urban exposure, and functional limitations shape later-life health and wellbeing.

I completed my Ph.D. in Economics at IIT Indore, and hold prior training in Population Studies from IIPS Mumbai and Geography from BHU and the University of Burdwan. Alongside academic work, I contribute to governance reforms and evaluation studies at NCGG.

Research

Migrants’ mental health

Depression risk in later-life migrants, focusing on age at migration, functional limitations, and social disadvantage.

Urban exposure & obesity

How timing, duration, and intensity of urban exposure shape obesity and cardiometabolic risk among middle- and older-aged migrants.

Hypertension & chronic disease

Rural-to-urban migration and onset of hypertension and multimorbidity in later life.

Migration, ageing & disparities

Comparisons of migrant and non-migrant older adults’ health using matching and decomposition methods.

Policy work

At NCGG, I contribute to evaluation and advisory work for Union and State governments in India. Recent engagements include:

  • Assessment of Right to Service (RTS) Acts in Haryana, Maharashtra, and Madhya Pradesh.
  • Analysis of digital service-delivery portals such as SARAL, Aaple Sarkar and MP e-District.
  • Support to evaluations of national skill-development and governance initiatives.

Data & methods

I routinely work with LASI, NFHS (DHS), IHDS, WHO-SAGE, NSSO, Census of India, and primary surveys.

  • OLS, logistic/probit, quantile regression, survival and hazard models.
  • Mediation and moderation, causal mediation, propensity score matching.
  • Decomposition, multilevel models, SEM, spatial analysis.
  • Stata (advanced), R (intermediate), spatial tools (GeoDA, ArcGIS).
