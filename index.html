Understanding how migration shapes later-life health and wellbeing.

I am an economist and population researcher working at the intersection of internal migration, ageing, and health inequalities in India. I combine large-scale survey data, life-course perspectives, and evidence from governance reforms to understand how mobility and place shape wellbeing in later life.

About

I am currently a Consultant (Grade I) at the National Centre for Good Governance (NCGG), Ministry of Personnel, Public Grievances and Pensions, Government of India, based in New Delhi. I work on the evaluation and strengthening of public service delivery systems, including Right to Service (RTS) Acts and digital governance portals across Indian states.

I completed my Ph.D. in Economics at the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Indore, Department of Humanities and Social Sciences, under the supervision of Dr. Kalandi Charan Pradhan. My doctoral research focuses on internal migration, urban exposure, and later-life health outcomes among older adults in India, using data from large-scale surveys such as LASI and other national datasets.

Research

Later-life migration & successful ageing

How migration histories and urban exposure shape physical health, functional limitations, depression, and subjective wellbeing among older adults in India.

Urban exposure & obesity

Examining how the timing, duration, and intensity of urban residence influence obesity and cardiometabolic risks among middle- and older-aged migrants.

Mental health & disability

Investigating how functional limitations, social exclusion, and access to care combine to produce inequalities in late-life depression.

Governance & service delivery

Evaluating RTS Acts, digital portals, and grievance redress systems using administrative data and mixed-methods approaches.

Conceptually, my work draws on life-course perspectives, cumulative (dis)advantage, and the capability approach, linking early- and mid-life opportunities to health and wellbeing in older age.
