About

I am currently a Consultant (Grade I) at the National Centre for Good Governance (NCGG), Ministry of Personnel, Public Grievances and Pensions, Government of India, based in New Delhi. I work on evaluation and strengthening of public service delivery systems, including Right to Service (RTS) Acts and digital governance portals across Indian states.

I recently completed my Ph.D. in Economics at the Indian Institute of Technology (IIT) Indore, Department of Humanities and Social Sciences, under the supervision of Dr. Kalandi Charan Pradhan. My doctoral research focuses on internal migration, urban exposure, and later-life health outcomes among older adults in India, using data from large-scale surveys such as LASI and other national datasets.

Broadly, my research interests include:

  • Internal migration and life-course trajectories
  • Ageing and health inequalities in low- and middle-income countries
  • Urban exposure, obesity, and cardiometabolic risks
  • Subjective wellbeing, mental health, and successful ageing
  • Governance and public service delivery in India

Research

Current Themes

  • Later-life migration and successful ageing: How migration histories and urban exposure shape physical health, functional limitations, depression, and subjective wellbeing among older adults in India.
  • Urban exposure and obesity: Investigating how the timing, duration, and intensity of urban residence influence obesity and related health risks among middle- and older-aged migrants.
  • Mental health and functional limitations: Examining the pathways through which functional disabilities and social exclusion affect depression among older migrants.
  • Governance and service delivery: Evaluating the implementation of Right to Service Acts, digital portals, and grievance redress systems in Indian states.

Theoretical & Conceptual Lens

My work draws on life-course perspectives, cumulative (dis)advantage, and capability approaches to understand how early-life and mid-life opportunities and constraints shape health and wellbeing in older age. I also engage with comparative frameworks linking migration, urbanisation, and inequalities in later life.

Policy & Consultancy Work

At the National Centre for Good Governance (NCGG), I contribute to evaluation studies and policy support for Union and State governments in India. My recent work includes:

  • Assessing the implementation and outcomes of Right to Service (RTS) Acts in states such as Haryana, Maharashtra, and Madhya Pradesh.
  • Analysing administrative data from digital service delivery portals (e.g., SARAL, Aaple Sarkar, MP e-District) to understand patterns in applications, approvals, delays, and grievance redressal.
  • Supporting evaluations of national skill development and public service schemes, including sampling design, survey tools, and analytical frameworks.

These engagements allow me to bridge rigorous academic research with practical governance reforms and contribute to evidence-based policymaking in India.
