Understanding how migration shapes later-life health and wellbeing.

I am a Fellow at the National Centre for Good Governance (NCGG), Ministry of Personnel, Public Grievances and Pensions, Government of India. My work sits at the intersection of internal migration, ageing, and health inequalities, with a focus on the Indian context.

I hold a Ph.D. in Economics from IIT Indore, and prior training in Population Studies at IIPS Mumbai and Geography at BHU and the University of Burdwan. My research uses large-scale ageing surveys such as LASI and NFHS to examine how life-course migration trajectories, urban exposure, and functional limitations shape health and wellbeing in older age.

Research

Migrants’ mental health

Depression risk in later-life migrants, focusing on age at migration, functional limitations, and social disadvantage.

Urban exposure & obesity

How timing, duration, and intensity of urban exposure shape obesity and cardiometabolic risk among middle- and older-aged migrants.

Hypertension & chronic disease

Rural-to-urban migration and onset of hypertension and multimorbidity in later life.

Migration, ageing & disparities

Comparing health outcomes of migrant and non-migrant older adults in India, using matching and decomposition methods.

Conceptually, my work draws on life-course, cumulative (dis)advantage, and capability approaches to link migration histories with health in later life.

Policy work

Data & methods

I routinely work with large-scale datasets such as LASI, NFHS (DHS), IHDS, WHO-SAGE, Census of India, and primary surveys over 500 respondents.

Methods and tools:

  • OLS, logistic/probit, quantile regression, hazard models.
  • Moderation and mediation, causal mediation, propensity score matching.
  • Decomposition techniques, multilevel modelling, structural equation models.
  • Stata (proficient), R (intermediate), spatial tools (GeoDA, ArcGIS).
