About

I am an economist and population researcher working at the intersection of internal migration, ageing, and health inequalities in India. My work combines large-scale survey data, life-course frameworks, and evidence from governance reforms to understand how mobility and place shape wellbeing in later life. Additionally, I am currently a Consultant at the National Centre for Good Governance (NCGG), Government of India. I work on the evaluation and strengthening of public service delivery systems, including Right to Service (RTS) Acts and governance reforms across Indian states.

I completed my Ph.D. in Economics at IIT Indore, focusing on internal migration, urban exposure, and later-life health outcomes among older adults in India.

Research

Later-life migration

How migration histories influence ageing, depression, and functional health.

Urban exposure & obesity

Understanding obesity risks across timing, duration, and intensity of urban residence.

Mental health & disability

Exploring how functional limitations and social exclusion shape late-life depression.

Governance & service delivery

Evaluating RTS Acts and digital service systems using administrative data.

Thematically grounded in life-course, cumulative (dis)advantage, and capability approaches.
